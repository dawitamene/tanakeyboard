# Prompt: Play Store screenshot images for Addiyon Keyboard

You are designing **promotional screenshot images** for the Google Play listing of
**Addiyon Keyboard**, an Amharic (Ge'ez/Fidel) Android keyboard with Latin-to-Fidel
transliteration. I will provide 4 raw phone screenshots — one per feature. Your job is to
compose each raw screenshot into a polished marketing image: the phone screenshot framed
inside a device mockup (or cleanly cropped), placed on a designed background with a short
headline and supporting text. The same 4 designs are then re-exported at tablet sizes.

## Hard technical requirements (Play Store rules)

All images: PNG or JPEG, max 8 MB each, **9:16 portrait** aspect ratio.

Produce **three sets** of the same 4 designs:

| Set | Size (9:16) | Play Store constraint |
|---|---|---|
| Phone (4 images) | **1080 × 1920 px** | Sides 320–3,840 px; min 1080 px per side required for promotion eligibility |
| 7-inch tablet (4 images) | **1080 × 1920 px** | Sides 320–3,840 px |
| 10-inch tablet (4 images) | **2160 × 3840 px** | Sides 1,080–7,680 px |

- The tablet sets reuse the same layouts and copy as the phone set. If I provide separate
  tablet screenshots, use those inside a tablet device frame; otherwise re-render the phone
  design at the target resolution (all assets must be vector/high-res so the 2160 × 3840
  export stays sharp — do not upscale a finished 1080 px image).
- All images must look like one consistent set (same background system, typography, and
  device framing) so they read as a series when swiped through on the store page.

## Design direction

- Clean, modern, friendly — this is a productivity/typing app, not a game
- Suggested palette: draw from the Ethiopian identity subtly (deep green, warm gold/yellow,
  accents of red) on a light or softly gradient background — tasteful, not flag-literal.
  Alternatively a single confident brand color across all slides. Pick one system and keep it.
- Headline at the top of each image, large and readable at thumbnail size (the store shows
  small previews); one short supporting line beneath it; phone screenshot below, slightly
  larger than half the image height, with a soft drop shadow
- Headlines should be bilingual-friendly: English headline, optionally a small Amharic
  word (e.g. ሰላም) used as a decorative/authentic accent
- No fake UI: the screenshot content is real — text and graphics you add live on the
  background around/above the device, never painted over the keyboard itself
- Leave safe margins: no text within ~64 px of any edge (scale proportionally at 2160 px)

## The 4 features (one per image, in this order)

### Image 1 — Type in Latin, get Amharic instantly
The hero slide. Screenshot shows the Amharic keyboard mid-word with the transliteration in
action (Latin buffer in the preview strip, Fidel appearing as the top suggestion).
- Headline: **"Type 'selam' — get ሰላም"**
- Support line: "Fast Latin-to-Fidel typing. No new layout to learn."

### Image 2 — Smart word suggestions
Screenshot shows the suggestion bar populated with Amharic word predictions while typing.
- Headline: **"Smart Amharic suggestions"**
- Support line: "182,000+ Amharic words predicted as you type."

### Image 3 — Voice input
Screenshot shows the keyboard with voice input active (mic engaged / listening state, or
dictated Amharic text appearing in the field).
- Headline: **"Speak it, we'll type it"**
- Support line: "Hands-free Amharic voice typing built in."

### Image 4 — English and Amharic in one keyboard
Screenshot shows the English layout (or the language-switch moment) with English
suggestions/autocorrect visible.
- Headline: **"Switch languages in one tap"**
- Support line: "Full English keyboard with suggestions, built in."
