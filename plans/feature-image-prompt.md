# Google Play Feature Image Prompt for Google Stitches

## Context (what the app is)

Tana Keyboard (branded "Addiyon") is an Android keyboard app that lets users type **Amharic** (the Ge'ez / Ethiopic script used in Ethiopia) by typing **Latin letters** on a standard QWERTY layout. It transliterates in real time — so typing "selam" produces ሰላም ("peace"). It also has a full English keyboard mode, switchable with one tap. The keyboard mimics the iOS keyboard look: rounded cards with subtle shadows, no Material ripple.

**Key visual elements of the app:**
- **Logo**: A Ge'ez character **አ** (the glottal "a") in warm white (#FFF6F0) on a vermillion-red (#EE4D2D) rounded square, with two horizontal bands across the background. The user will provide `logo.svg`.
- **Brand colors**: vermillion red (#EE4D2D, #C63A1E), warm white (#FFF6F0), ink/charcoal (#17150F, #2A2620), paper/sand (#F4F1EA, #E4DFD3), stone (#8A8578)
- **The keyboard itself** looks like an iOS keyboard: white rounded-rect keys with subtle shadow, letter keys lighter, special keys (shift, space, enter) darker gray. The keyboard has a suggestion strip at top showing Amharic words in fidel script.
- **Space bar** shows "አማርኛ" (Amharic for "Amharic") when in Amharic mode.
- **Language toggle key** shows ሀለ stacked above AB with a divider line.
- **Corner fidel previews**: each letter key shows a tiny Ge'ez character in its top-right corner (e.g., the "h" key shows ህ).
- **Key Amharic characters** seen on screen: ሰላም, አማርኛ, አ, ሀ, ለ, ሐ, መ, ሰ, ቀ, በ, ተ, ቸ, ነ, ከ, ወ, ዘ, የ, ደ, ገ, ጠ, ጨ, ጰ, ጸ, ፈ, ፐ

## Target format

**Google Play Feature Image**: 1024 × 500 pixels, PNG or JPEG. This is the hero banner at the top of the Play Store listing. No small text, no device frames required, no "screenshot" label. It should communicate the app's value proposition at a glance.

## Visual composition guidelines

- **Left side (or center)**: The app logo (from logo.svg), large enough to be recognizable. The logo is a vermillion-red rounded square with a white አ glyph.
- **Right side (or behind/alongside)**: A stylized representation of the keyboard itself, showing Amharic fidel characters on keys. This could be a realistic phone keyboard render, a flat illustration of the key layout, or a floating composition of Ge'ez characters.
- **App name**: The wordmark should use **Playpen Sans ExtraBold** if possible, or a friendly rounded sans-serif. Primary text in vermillion (#EE4D2D) or charcoal (#2A2620). Show either "Addiyon Keyboard" or "Tana Keyboard" prominently.
- **Tagline**: Small secondary text below the name, in stone (#8A8578) or charcoal at lower opacity. Suggested: "Type Amharic naturally — in Latin" or "Amharic + English keyboard" or similar.
- **Background**: Warm, earthy, paper-like (use colors from the brand palette: #F4F1EA paper tone with a subtle gradient). Could include a subtle sine-wave decorative element near the bottom (evoking the app's "Paper" background effect), in vermillion at very low opacity (~8%).
- **Mood**: Clean, modern, warm, professional. Ethiopian cultural identity conveyed through the script itself and the warm earthy palette — not through clip-art or literal cultural imagery.

## Content checklist for the image

1. **App logo** (from the provided logo.svg) — placed left of center or at center
2. **App name** "Addiyon Keyboard" or "Tana Keyboard" in large, readable type
3. **A visual of the keyboard** showing Ge'ez characters on keys — either rendered realistically or as a stylized flat composition
4. **A short tagline** in smaller text
5. **Amharic script visible** — at least 3–5 distinct Ge'ez characters (ሰላም, አማርኛ, አ, etc.)
6. **Dual-language hint** — some visual cue that the keyboard does both Amharic and English (e.g., the space bar showing "አማርኛ" next to "English", or the ሀለ/AB toggle button)

## Style guidance

- **Do**: Use the Ge'ez script as the primary visual motif — it is beautiful and distinctive on its own. Clean typography, generous whitespace, warm minimalism.
- **Do**: Show the keyboard or key layout so people immediately understand this is a keyboard app.
- **Do**: Keep the logo prominent — brand recognition matters.
- **Don't**: Use Ethiopian-flag colors literally (green/yellow/red stripes). Let the brand palette carry the identity.
- **Don't**: Include device frames/bezels unless they are very minimal — the feature image should focus on the keyboard and branding, not a phone mockup.
- **Don't**: Use overly busy patterns, clip-art lions, traditional cross motifs, or other touristy Ethiopian imagery. The script is the identity.

## Alternate approach (simpler / more modern)

If a realistic keyboard render is too complex, use:
- A large, dramatic **አ** (or a short word like **ሰላም**) as a hero glyph, rendered huge and semi-transparent in vermillion behind the logo + name
- Floating Ge'ez characters in different sizes and opacities arranged around the logo, giving an abstract typographic feel
- The tagline and app name cleanly overlaid
